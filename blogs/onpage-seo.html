What Is On-Page SEO?

On-page SEO refers to all the optimisations you make directly on a webpage to improve its visibility in search results. Unlike link building, on-page SEO is entirely within your control.

Title Tags

  • Include your primary keyword near the beginning
  • Keep it under 60 characters to avoid truncation
  • Write for clicks — make it compelling, not keyword-stuffed
  • Every page should have a unique title tag

Meta Descriptions

  • Keep them under 160 characters
  • Include your primary keyword naturally
  • Write a clear, benefit-led summary of what the page delivers
  • Treat it like ad copy — it should earn the click

Headings (H1, H2, H3)

  • Use exactly one H1 per page — it should reflect the title tag
  • Use H2 tags for main sections and H3 for sub-sections
  • Include related keywords in headings naturally — never force them

Images

  • Add descriptive alt text to every image
  • Compress images before uploading (Squoosh or ShortPixel)
  • Use modern formats like WebP where possible
  • Set explicit width and height attributes to prevent layout shift

Internal Linking & URLs

  • Link to related articles naturally within the body copy
  • Use descriptive anchor text — avoid "click here"
  • Keep URLs short and descriptive: /on-page-seo-checklist
  • Use hyphens to separate words, not underscores

Content Quality

Google rewards content that demonstrates experience, expertise, authoritativeness and trustworthiness (E-E-A-T). Before publishing, ensure your page fully answers the reader's question, is more thorough than what currently ranks, and is easy to read with short paragraphs and clear headings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about on-page SEO, answered clearly.

How many keywords should I target on a single page?+

Focus on one primary keyword per page, supported by a handful of closely related secondary keywords and natural variations. Trying to rank for too many unrelated terms dilutes a page's focus.

A better approach: create separate, dedicated pages for distinct keyword targets, then use internal links to connect them.

Does the meta description affect rankings directly?+

No — Google has confirmed that meta descriptions are not a direct ranking factor. However, a well-written meta description improves your click-through rate (CTR), which means more organic traffic.

Google may also rewrite your meta description if it decides a different snippet better matches a particular search query.

How long should a blog post be for SEO?+

There is no magic word count. The right length is however long it takes to fully answer the user's question. Padding an article with filler content to hit an arbitrary word count actively harms quality.

A practical benchmark: check the average length of the top 3–5 results for your target keyword. Match or slightly exceed their depth.

What is keyword stuffing and how do I avoid it?+

Keyword stuffing is overloading a page with keywords in an unnatural way. Google's algorithms identify and penalise this behaviour.

Avoid it by writing for your reader first. Use your primary keyword naturally where it fits, and use synonyms and related phrases throughout. If it reads awkwardly aloud, it probably contains too many forced keywords.

Should I optimise old blog posts or just publish new ones?+

Both — but refreshing existing content is often the faster win. Pages that are already indexed and have some authority can see quick ranking improvements when updated with fresh information and better structure.

Prioritise posts that rank on page 2 or in positions 8–15. These are close to the top and often just need minor improvements to break into the top five.

What is E-E-A-T and does it affect my rankings?+

E-E-A-T stands for Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness and Trustworthiness. It is the framework Google's quality raters use to evaluate content quality, particularly in health, finance and legal topics.

To build E-E-A-T: publish accurate, well-sourced content, include author bios that demonstrate credentials, earn links from reputable sites, and keep your content up to date.
